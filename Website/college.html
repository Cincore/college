Computer Science Program

Georgia Southern has a pretty nice overall Computer Science Department. Georgia Southern University’s Computer Science Department is part of the state-of-the-art College of Engineering and Computing and offers Bachelor of Science and Master of Science degrees. class sizes are small and research opportunities abound. Active research areas in the department include game programming, data and knowledge systems, broadband networking, computer security, mobile computing, robotics and software engineering. According to the United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, computer science related jobs are among the top ten fastest growing careers that show a lot of promise and opportunities for growth.

Benefits

Some benefits can be developing the ability to apply knowledge of computing and mathematics appropriate to the discipline, ability to analyze a problem, and identify and define the computing requirements appropriate to its solution, ability to design, implement and evaluate a computer‐based system, process, component, or program to meet desired needs, and many more. Even though it is not listed, I still personally believe GSU is a great place to study Computer Science. Yes it will be tough, but I believe I can handle it.

Average Salary Post Graduation

At GSU, the Bachelor of Science (BS/BSc) average salary is around $54,046. Independently, that can be a solid place to start.
